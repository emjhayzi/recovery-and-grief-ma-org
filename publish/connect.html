Individual outreach · Peer Grief Support Services

Through our outreach programs — Grief Connections and Finding Your Way — grieving people are contacted by a Peer Grief Helper who listens with compassion and gives information and support according to each person’s particular needs.

Grief Connections works with local agencies to reach out and provide timely, personalized assistance to grievers. Finding Your Way responds to individuals who contact SADOD directly.

Get Ongoing Help

One-on-one support · Peer Grief Ally Program

Through the Peer Grief Ally Program, a bereaved person and their Ally work together on the issues most important to the griever. Allies are not clinicians: they are volunteers trained to support grieving people by having conversations about what each person most needs and wants to talk about.

The tragedies suffered by the Ally and the grieving person create common ground that is the foundation for a mutually healing experience. If you are interested in volunteering as a Peer Grief Ally, learn how you can help.

About our outreach programs

Our outreach programs are described below. We have two programs for you to consider: Grief Connections and the Peer Grief Ally Program. Staff and volunteers both take part in outreach to grievers, and everyone involved has experienced the pain and suffering — we are here to help people as they navigate their own grief journey.

We are not clinicians or professional counselors. We are Peer Grief Helpers who provide compassionate support to grieving people for a short period of time to help them find their way. We also have volunteers, called Peer Grief Allies, who offer grievers ongoing, one-on-one peer grief support.

What it means to connect with a Peer Grief Helper

By connecting with a Peer Grief Helper, you will be able to tell your story to someone who knows what the pain of this kind of grief is like — and who will listen without opinions, pressure, or judgment. Peer Grief Helpers have traveled their grief journey for a year or more and, from their own experience, can relate to how difficult it is to cope with loss after a loved one dies from substance use. Peer Grief Helpers have found hope and healing, and they also understand what it is like when grief is overwhelming. They meet you where you are on your grief journey.

Peer Grief Helpers give you reassurance that you are not alone. Each of them has found their way to the other side of grief and will help you find your personal path to healing.
