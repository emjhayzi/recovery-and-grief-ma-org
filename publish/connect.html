Direct Connection

Connect With a Peer Grief Helper

Reaching out should feel simple and low-pressure. This page is designed to help you get support, ask questions, and understand what kinds of one-on-one connection may be available.

02A

Get Support and Information

If you want a low-barrier first step, direct connection is a good place to begin. You can ask questions, learn what support options exist, and get help figuring out what may fit best right now.

  • Text-first outreach can be prioritized
  • You can start with one question or one short message
  • A real person can help you navigate next steps

02B

Get One-on-One Help

Ongoing one-on-one support is intended to connect a grieving person with a trained peer helper for individualized support over time. This section can be expanded as the matching process, intake details, and available helpers are finalized.

  • Personalized support from someone with lived experience
  • Space to talk through grief at your own pace
  • A clearer bridge into longer-term peer connection

What happens after someone reaches out?

The first goal is connection, not paperwork. A simple reply can help someone feel seen, understand their options, and move toward the kind of support that fits their needs.
