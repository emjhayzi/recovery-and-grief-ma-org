About Us

Learn About Peer Grief Support

We are people in recovery who understand the pain of grieving a death caused by alcohol or other drugs. This support is rooted in shared experience, compassion, and the belief that no one should have to carry grief in isolation.

Our invitation to you

If you have experienced the death of someone you care about from alcohol or other drugs, our services are for you. Whatever your relationship was with that person, and wherever you are on your recovery journey, you are welcome here.

What we do

We offer direct connection, one-on-one pathways, grief support groups, whole-person support options, and carefully chosen resources. Each option is intended to help you move toward support in a way that feels manageable.

How peer grief support works

Peer grief support is mutual aid in a safe space. It is not clinical treatment. It is grieving people helping grieving people through presence, understanding, and shared experience.
