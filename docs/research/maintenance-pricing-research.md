# Website Maintenance Pricing Research

Reference note reviewed on April 12, 2026.

This file is supporting market research only. It is not a source of truth for website implementation state.

Market snapshot prepared for positioning website maintenance services.

Date of snapshot: March 25, 2026

## Important Context

Most publicly listed maintenance pricing online is still centered around WordPress websites.

That matters because a custom static website usually has:

- fewer moving parts
- fewer plugin or update issues
- lower security overhead
- simpler hosting needs

So the market prices below are useful anchors, but a static site can often justify:

- a lower technical-maintenance base fee
- while still supporting higher-priced plans when content updates, design refinements, SEO, reporting, and strategy are included

## Public Market Pricing Snapshot

### Low-End / Basic Maintenance

Public examples:

- OnSiteWP: `$60/month` basic maintenance plan
- WPCaps: `$79/month` basic plan
- WP Buffs: `$89/month` maintain plan
- Fixmysite: `$99/month` maintenance plan

What this level usually includes:

- updates
- backups
- uptime/security monitoring
- very limited small fixes
- email support

Interpretation:

The lower end of the visible market for simple maintenance sits around:

- `$60 to $100/month`

This is usually a "keep the lights on" level, not an active growth partnership.

### Mid-Tier / Small Business Support

Public examples:

- OnSiteWP: `$120/month`
- WPCaps: `$149/month`
- WP Buffs: `$179/month`
- Fixmysite: `$159/month`
- COVA Services: `$120 for 2 hours`, `$225 for 4 hours`, `$300 for 6 hours`

What this level usually includes:

- stronger monitoring
- minor content edits
- more support time
- light performance work
- monthly reporting

Interpretation:

The visible market for small-business support plans clusters around:

- `$120 to $300/month`

This is the range where clients start to expect not just maintenance, but small ongoing changes and quicker support.

### Higher-Tier / Active Retainer

Public examples:

- OnSiteWP: `$299/month`
- WP Buffs: `$239 to $359/month` depending on complexity
- Fixmysite: `$239 to $499/month`

What this level usually includes:

- faster response
- more hands-on support
- performance optimization
- custom functionality support
- heavier security or e-commerce support

Interpretation:

The visible public market for stronger support retainers begins around:

- `$250/month`

and often goes to:

- `$500/month` and beyond

especially when the service includes custom development, strategy, SEO, design iteration, or urgent support.

### Hourly Pricing

Public examples:

- WebCitz: `$119.50/hour` invoiced monthly
- WebCitz: as low as `$99.95/hour` with prepaid time
- COVA Services: `$50/hour` for additional hours beyond package time

Interpretation:

A practical public-market hourly band is roughly:

- `$50 to $120/hour`

Lower rates tend to be tied to overflow hours on a package or very small-scope support.
Higher rates usually reflect agency support, consulting, or specialized development.

## Practical Market Read

For a small-business or nonprofit-style informational website, the market currently supports:

- `$75 to $150/month` for basic maintenance
- `$150 to $300/month` for meaningful ongoing support
- `$300 to $750+/month` for maintenance plus growth, iteration, and higher-touch service
- `$75 to $125/hour` for ad-hoc work, depending on experience and scope

This range is an inference from current public pricing and is especially relevant for brochure sites, service-business websites, and custom sites with moderate ongoing needs.

## Recommended Pricing Strategy For This Project Type

Because this website is a custom static site rather than a plugin-heavy CMS build, a strong offer could look like this:

### Option 1: Light Care Plan

Suggested price:

- `$95/month`

Suggested positioning:

- monthly check-in
- broken-link check
- uptime check
- one small content update batch
- email support
- standard turnaround

Best for:

- clients who mostly need peace of mind and occasional edits

### Option 2: Ongoing Support Plan

Suggested price:

- `$175 to $225/month`

Suggested positioning:

- everything in Light Care
- more content/design updates
- form monitoring
- monthly website review
- faster turnaround
- small front-end tweaks included

Best for:

- clients who expect regular updates and want you involved monthly

### Option 3: Growth / Partnership Retainer

Suggested price:

- `$350 to $600/month`

Suggested positioning:

- maintenance plus proactive improvement
- landing page changes
- design refinements
- SEO updates
- analytics review
- strategic support

Best for:

- clients who want the site to keep improving, not just stay online

### Option 4: Ad-Hoc Rate

Suggested price:

- `$85 to $125/hour`

Suggested positioning:

- one-off fixes
- content uploads
- design tweaks
- emergency support

Best for:

- clients who do not want a monthly plan
- occasional extra work outside retainer scope

## Positioning Advice

If you sell "maintenance" only, clients often compare you to low-cost commodity support.

If you sell:

- maintenance
- content support
- design upkeep
- reporting
- strategic recommendations

then you move into a stronger-value category and can price closer to the mid or upper part of the market.

For this specific project type, a good default offer would likely be:

- `$175 to $225/month` for a standard recurring support plan

That feels credible against current market pricing while still being easier to justify than a more agency-like retainer.
