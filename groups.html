Support Groups

Attend a Grief Support Group

Group support can offer a steady place to listen, speak, and be around others who understand this kind of grief. This page is meant to make group participation feel clear and approachable.

What happens in a group?

People gather in a respectful, peer-led space to talk about grief, hear from others, and remember that they are not alone. Sharing is welcomed, and listening is enough if that is what feels right.

What to expect

Groups are designed to be compassionate, non-judgmental, and recovery-affirming. The tone should feel grounded rather than clinical, with room for different recovery paths and grief experiences.
